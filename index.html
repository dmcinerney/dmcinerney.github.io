My name is Denis Jered McInerney (I go by Jered). I am a Ph.D. Candidate at Northeastern University in Computer Science, advised by Byron Wallace and Jan-Willem van de Meent and graduating in Spring 2024.

I am interested in finding ways we can use machine learning and natural language processing as a tool to access information that can help us make more informed and efficient decisions. On the technical side, I love working with “naturally-occuring” data in settings with little-to-no supervision. My current research focuses finding and presenting information to clinicians that can help them make a diagnosis. In particular, I am looking at apply large language models and multi-modal pretrained models to form a helpful interface to the electronic health records of patients.

In my spare time, I love playing tennis and running (short distances), rock climbing, and playing tug-of-war with my dog. I am also a fan of singing in the car to shake off the a capella rust (check out Johns Hopkins Ketzev)!

I am currently searching for both jobs and post-doctoral opportunities to start after I graduate.
